Positioning Bet

How we stand out among four featured agencies — and why "skin in the game" beats "more views than them."

Prepared May 22 2026 · Thienvu, Daniel, Danny
The setup

We just bought a seat at the table.
Now we have five seconds to differentiate.

$3K/month for the SideShift Discover featured row, plus 10% on every deal that closes through the platform. We are now one of four agencies a sophisticated buyer sees side-by-side on the same page. If our card doesn't hit different in five seconds, this is dead money.

The question isn't "how do we look like a good UGC agency?"
It's "how do we be the only obvious pick when the buyer compares us to four others?"

The trap

Don't play the
view-counting game.

Plutus claims 2B+. Social Launchpad claims 1B+. Ki Marketing claims 600M+. DTC claims 1B+. If we try to win on raw numbers, we're competitor #5 at best — and the buyer's eyes glaze over anyway because everyone's playing the same instrument.

The asymmetric move isn't a bigger number.
It's a different kind of claim entirely.
Where everyone is — and where nobody is

Map them on the axes that actually matter.

Forget view counts. Look at the dimensions a sophisticated buyer cares about: how much it costs, how fast you ship, what happens if it doesn't work, and who's actually doing the work.

Insight #1

Native UGC
is the new AI-native.

In 2024–25, "AI-native" became cultural shorthand for built from the ground up for this category — not bolted onto a legacy business. Cluely, Cursor, Perplexity vs. Adobe-with-AI. Sophisticated buyers transfer the same mental model anywhere they shop.

The other four

Marketing agency
+ UGC product line

Discovery phases. Strategy decks. Content-brief retainers. Creative-direction departments. UGC wrapped in consulting overhead. The video is one deliverable among many.

VoC

Native UGC studio
built by working creators

No discovery phase. No strategy deck. No content-brief retainer. The deliverable is videos. The operators are creators with their own audiences. First posts live in 7–10 days.

The honest pressure test

Ki Marketing has a creator-first claim too.
We need a sharper distinction.

Jonathan Ki was a 100M-view creator before building the agency. He literally built SideShift's creator training course. By a loose definition of "native," Ki has a real claim. If a buyer asks Claude "which is most creator-native?", we may not be the only answer.

The sharper claim

UGC-pure, not UGC-plus-strategy.

Every other featured agency — Ki included — sells strategy briefs, content roadmaps, or creative-direction work alongside the videos. VoC's deliverable is 180 videos. Period. Output, not overhead. That's a knife only our offer mechanics back up.

Insight #2

The buyer has
two readers now.

In 2026, sophisticated B2B buyers screenshot the row of agencies and paste it into Claude/GPT: "Which of these fits my $10K AI consumer app build?" Our card has to be parseable by the human and the LLM. Vague taglines die in that comparison.

What the human reads
"Native UGC for AI startups & consumer apps — built by working creators. $10K/month. 50% back if we don't hit 1M views in Month 1."
What Claude parses
category: native UGC
audience: AI startups consumer apps
price: $10,000/mo
volume: 180 videos 4 platforms
guarantee: 50% refund <1M views
speed: 7–10 days first video
proof: Cluely Codedex Fable

Every claim has to be a number, a named entity, or a literal category. Adjectives evaporate. "Premium," "world-class," "best-in-class" — discounted to zero by the LLM and skipped by the human.

The bet

Three wedges. One offer.

"Native UGC for AI startups & consumer apps — built by working creators. First videos in 7–10 days. 50% refund if we don't hit 1M views in Month 1."
1.
Category claim

Native UGC, not marketing agency with UGC. Sets the frame the buyer evaluates everything else through.

2.
Speed claim

First videos in 7–10 days. Plutus is still in "discovery → strategy" in week 4. Tangible operational difference.

3.
Consequence claim

50% refund if <1M views Month 1. The only featured agency with cash on the line. Plutus explicitly refuses guarantees.

The refund clause isn't a marketing flourish — it's defensible because the system already produces these results. Five named clients, all verifiable. No "anonymous Finance App, 700M views" stuff that makes sophisticated buyers cringe.

Discipline

What we deliberately leave out.

A sharp position is defined as much by what it refuses as by what it claims. These are the temptations we should resist.

✕
"The AI UGC agency"
Locks out Fable, Codedex, Makon. DTC already owns "AI/SaaS." Own the terms, not the niche.
✕
Billion-view bragging
Plutus (2B+) and Social Launchpad (1B+) will outclaim us on volume. Don't play a game you can't win.
✕
"Mentorship-turned-agency"
Too inside-baseball for a cold card. Powerful on a sales call — invisible to a scanner.
✕
"Cheaper than DTC"
"Cheap" reads as low quality. Position as right-priced because we don't carry consulting overhead.
